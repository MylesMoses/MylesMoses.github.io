Comparing Dinosaurs

Learn about the fascinating differences between avian and non-avian dinosaurs.

Differences

  • Extinction

    Non-avian dinosaurs became extinct after the Cretaceous–Paleogene extinction event, while avian dinosaurs mostly survived and evolved into modern-day birds.

  • Bone Structure

    Avian dinosaurs had hollow bones to aid in flight, whereas non-avian dinosaurs had dense bones to support their weight.

  • Metabolic Processes

    Non-avian dinosaurs were cold-blooded, unlike avian dinosaurs, which were warm-blooded and had different metabolic processes.

  • Feathers and Flight

    Avian dinosaurs, or birds, evolved from small, feathered theropod dinosaurs and are characterized by their feathers, which are used for insulation, display, and flight. Most non-avian dinosaurs did not have feathers and lacked the adaptations necessary for powered flight.

  • Brain Size and Behavior

    Birds have relatively large brains compared to their body size, which is evident in their complex behaviors and intelligence. Non-avian dinosaurs had varying brain sizes, with some like Troodon having relatively large brains, while others had smaller brains relative to their body size.

  • Reproduction

    Birds lay eggs and exhibit elaborate courtship displays, nest building, and parental care. Non-avian dinosaurs also laid eggs, but their reproductive behaviors likely differed, with some building nests and others possibly leaving their eggs unattended.

Did You Know?

Some theories suggest that avian dinosaurs survived due to their larger brains and varied diets compared to their non-avian counterparts.
